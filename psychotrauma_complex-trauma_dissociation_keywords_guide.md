## 15) Practical Tips

### PubMed
- Combine **MeSH terms** with **Title/Abstract terms**.
- Use `[tiab]` for flexible keyword searching.
- Use quotation marks for exact phrases.

### Google Scholar
- Keep searches shorter and more conceptual.
- Try multiple versions of the same concept.
- Add terms like `review`, `meta-analysis`, `systematic review`, or `randomized trial` when needed.

### Scopus / Web of Science
- Use field tags such as `TITLE-ABS-KEY`.
- Use phrase searching for terms like `"complex PTSD"` and `"peritraumatic dissociation"`.
- Add exclusion terms if surgical or injury-related trauma papers dominate results.

## 21) Final Note

The most efficient strategy is usually:
1. start broad,
2. split into trauma / complex trauma / dissociation subgroups,
3. add developmental or childhood trauma terms when relevant,
4. add treatment or assessment terms depending on your goal,
5. use exclusion terms when medical injury-related trauma papers dilute results.
